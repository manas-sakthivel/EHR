Security in the proposed Electronic Health Records (EHR) system is enforced using a defense-in-depth strategy, where multiple independent security mechanisms operate in tandem to protect sensitive medical data. Maintaining overall system integrity and policy enforcement

Critically, administrative actions are also subject to blockchain logging. This ensures that even the most privileged role cannot act without leaving a permanent and auditable trail.

Doctor / Laboratory

Doctors and laboratories act as authorized data producers and verifiers, with permissions restricted to:

Uploading medical files for patients under their authorized scope

Verifying file integrity against blockchain-stored hashes

Viewing only those medical records they have uploaded themselves

View all medical records associated with their identity

Independently verify the authenticity of their records

All role permissions are encoded directly in the smart contract logic. As a result, even a malicious backend, forged request, or compromised server cannot bypass authorization rules. Every attempted and successful operation emits blockchain events, enabling complete accountability and forensic traceability.
